Our Story

Rooted in Sooke, Inspired by Bees.

Welcome to Ella Park Honey

At Ella Park Honey, we are more than just beekeepers; we are custodians of nature, passionate about the incredible world of honeybees and the pristine environment of Sooke. Our journey began with a simple love for these amazing pollinators and a desire to share their gifts with our community.

With 15 thriving hives, we practice sustainable and ethical beekeeping, ensuring our bees are healthy, happy, and have ample access to the diverse local flora that makes Sooke honey so special. Our philosophy is simple: work in harmony with nature, prioritize bee welfare, and produce the purest, most delicious honey possible.

A Hands-On Adventure

Ever wondered what it's like inside a beehive? Join us for an unforgettable Beekeeping Experience Day at Ella Park Honey! This is a unique opportunity to don a bee suit, get up close and personal with our gentle bees, and learn directly from experienced beekeepers.

What you'll experience:

  • Learn about bee biology, hive society, and the importance of pollinators.
  • Safely open a hive and observe the colony in action.
  • Identify the queen, workers, and drones.
  • Hold a frame of bees and honeycomb (if you're comfortable!).
  • Understand the basics of honey extraction (seasonal).
  • Ask all your buzzing questions!

Our experiences are perfect for curious individuals, families, aspiring beekeepers, or anyone looking for a unique connection with nature. We provide all necessary protective gear and ensure a safe, educational, and fun environment.

The Amazing World of Honeybees

Discover Fascinating Facts About Our Pollinators.

Honeybees are vital pollinators, responsible for about one-third of the food we eat. They play a crucial role in maintaining biodiversity by pollinating countless species of wildflowers, trees, and crops. Without bees, our ecosystems and food supply would be drastically different, affecting everything from the fruits and vegetables we enjoy to the health of our natural landscapes.

A honeybee colony is a highly organized superorganism. It consists of a single queen bee (responsible for laying all the eggs, up to 2,000 a day!), thousands of sterile female worker bees (who forage for nectar and pollen, build comb, care for young, clean, and defend the hive), and, seasonally, a few hundred male drone bees (whose main purpose is to mate with a virgin queen from another colony). Each bee has a specific role that changes with age and contributes to the survival of the colony.

Worker bees collect nectar from flowers using their long, tube-like tongues (proboscis). They store it in a special "honey stomach" (crop), separate from their digestive stomach, where enzymes begin to break down complex sugars into simpler ones (glucose and fructose). Back at the hive, they regurgitate the nectar to other "house" bees who further process it by adding more enzymes and reducing its water content. This processed nectar is then deposited into honeycomb cells. The bees fan their wings vigorously to evaporate excess water, transforming the nectar into rich, stable honey, which they cap with beeswax for storage.

At Ella Park Honey, we prioritize the health and well-being of our bees above all else. We practice natural and sustainable beekeeping methods, avoid harsh chemicals and antibiotics, ensure our bees have diverse forage in the Sooke area, and never over-harvest honey, always leaving ample stores for the bees themselves, especially for winter. We believe that happy, healthy bees produce the best honey and are essential for a thriving local environment. Our practices focus on minimizing stress to the colonies and supporting their natural behaviors.
