Welcome to Ella Park Honey

Nestled in the heart of beautiful Sooke, British Columbia, Ella Park Honey is more than just a honey farm – it's a passion project dedicated to the incredible world of bees. We believe in sustainable practices, nurturing our 15 hives with care, and sharing the sweet rewards and fascinating knowledge with our community.

From the rich, flavourful honey harvested from local flora to immersive beekeeping experiences and our one-of-a-kind "Sleep Over Beehives" Airbnb, we invite you to connect with nature and discover the magic of these vital pollinators.

About Ella Park Honey

Our Story: A Passion for Bees

Ella Park Honey began as a small dream rooted in a deep appreciation for nature and the incredible world of honeybees. Located in the stunning natural landscape of Sooke, British Columbia, we started with just a few hives and a desire to learn. Today, with 15 thriving hives, our passion has grown into a small family-run endeavor focused on sustainable beekeeping, education, and sharing the unique gifts of the hive.

We believe that bees are essential to our ecosystem, and our practices reflect this belief. We prioritize the health and well-being of our bees above all, ensuring they have ample forage from the diverse local flora that Sooke offers. This commitment not only supports healthy bee populations but also results in honey that is a true reflection of our beautiful surroundings.

The Sooke Environment: Our Secret Ingredient

The unique character of Ella Park Honey is intrinsically linked to our location in Sooke. Surrounded by lush forests, coastal wildflowers, and diverse gardens, our bees have access to a rich tapestry of nectar and pollen sources throughout the seasons. This natural bounty contributes to the complex flavors and exceptional quality of our honey.

From the delicate notes of early spring blossoms to the robust flavors of late summer wildflowers, each harvest tells a story of the Sooke landscape. We are proud to offer a product that is not just honey, but a taste of the wild, untamed beauty of Vancouver Island's West Coast.

Our Pure Sooke Honey

From Our 15 Hives to Your Table

At Ella Park Honey, we lovingly care for 15 beehives, each a bustling community producing nature's sweetest gift. Our honey is raw, unpasteurized, and minimally filtered to preserve its natural enzymes, pollen, and delicate flavors. We believe in offering honey as pure as the bees make it.

Our Honey Varieties

While every season brings unique nuances, our primary honey offerings include:

  • Sooke Wildflower Medley: A delightful blend of nectar from the diverse wildflowers blooming throughout Sooke, offering a complex, ever-changing taste.
  • Coastal Heather Bloom (Seasonal): When conditions allow, a richer, more aromatic honey from the coastal heather that dots our landscape.
  • Summer Harvest Gold: A vibrant, golden honey capturing the essence of summer blossoms, perfect for everyday enjoyment.

The journey from hive to jar is one we undertake with immense care, ensuring the final product is of the highest quality. We hand-extract and bottle our honey in small batches on-site.

Why Choose Local, Raw Honey?

Supporting local beekeepers like Ella Park Honey means you're getting a product that's fresh, traceable, and supports your local ecosystem. Raw honey is packed with natural goodness that processed honey often lacks.

Where to Find Our Honey

Our honey is currently available for purchase directly from our farm by appointment and at select local Sooke markets. Please contact us for current availability and locations.

Beekeeping Experience Days

Immerse yourself in the fascinating world of bees with our hands-on Beekeeping Experience Days!

Discover the Sweet Secrets of the Hive

Our Beekeeping Experience Days are designed for anyone curious about bees – no prior experience necessary! Join us for an engaging and educational adventure where you'll get up close and personal with our gentle honeybees.

What You'll Do & Learn:
  • Don a protective beekeeping suit and learn how to safely approach and open a hive.
  • Observe the intricate workings of a bee colony: find the queen, see worker bees in action, and spot drones.
  • Learn about the bee life cycle, hive hierarchy, and how bees make honey.
  • Understand the importance of pollinators and what you can do to help them.
  • Enjoy a guided honey tasting session, sampling the unique flavors of Ella Park Honey.

Who is it for? Individuals, couples, families (children typically 8+ with adult supervision), and anyone with a budding interest in bees and nature.

What's included? All necessary protective gear, expert guidance, and light refreshments.

Booking Information: To inquire about availability, pricing, and to book your Beekeeping Experience Day, please contact us.

Unique Stays: Our "Bee & Bee"

Experience a buzzworthy getaway unlike any other at Ella Park Honey's "Sleep Over Beehives" Airbnb!

Sleep Soundly by the Gentle Hum of Bees

Imagine a tranquil retreat where you can safely and comfortably connect with nature and the fascinating world of honeybees. Our unique Airbnb offers just that – a cozy, self-contained cabin thoughtfully designed for a peaceful stay, located in close proximity to our apiary.

The Experience:

While maintaining a safe distance, you'll have the unique opportunity to observe the comings and goings of our bees and listen to their gentle hum. It's a profoundly relaxing and educational experience, perfect for nature lovers, writers, artists, or anyone seeking a serene escape.

Accommodation Highlights:
  • Cozy and private cabin with comfortable sleeping arrangements.
  • Observation window (safely screened) offering glimpses into the apiary area (seasonal activity).
  • Eco-friendly amenities and a focus on sustainable comfort.
  • Private entrance and outdoor seating area to enjoy the Sooke air.
  • Easy access to local Sooke attractions and natural beauty.

Safety First: Your safety and comfort, as well as the well-being of our bees, are paramount. The accommodation is designed to provide a unique experience without direct interaction with the hives unless part of a booked Beekeeping Experience.
